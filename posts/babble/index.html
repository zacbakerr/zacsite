The Library of Babble

    The Library of Babble contains everything that has been written, will be written, and could be written. Well, at least things with a length of less than 856. The possible characters are the lowercase alphabet, the comma, the period, and the space. So how many books are there in this athenaeum? Well with 29 possible characters and ~855 characters in one book, there are 29^855 books. Or if you prefer a visual representation of how vast this athenaeum is:

    This is about 1,170 decimal places more than the number of atoms in the universe. So yeah, there are a lot of combinations. How exactly was this done? Well, when searching the athenaeum, the user makes a variety of decisions including:

  1. Which galaxy in the universe to visit
  2. Which solar system in that galaxy they would like to go to
  3. What planet they would like to land on
  4. Which library they wish to explore
  5. What section of the library they would like to venture into
  6. Which shelf piques their interest
  7. What series to investigate
  8. And ultimately, which book to read

    Each decision comes in the form of 20 randomly chosen characters, although users are encouraged to enter non-random inputs for each step of the way. All of these inputs are joined and convereted to binary. Then a series of bitwise operations are preformed on the binary string. It is then fed through the Halton Sequence, converted back to binary, then to decimal (in different sized chunks), and then convereted to charecters using the Unicode Standard.
